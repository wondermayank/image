Frequently Asked Questions

What is WebP?

WebP is an image format developed by Google that provides superior compression for web images. WebP files are typically 25–35% smaller than JPEG and 26% smaller than PNG at equivalent visual quality.

Should I use lossy or lossless?

Lossy is best for photographs — it produces much smaller files with minimal visible quality loss. Lossless is ideal for graphics, icons, and images where every pixel must be preserved.

Do all browsers support WebP?

Yes — all modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari 14+, Edge) support WebP. For older browsers, keep a JPEG/PNG fallback.

What quality should I use?

Quality 75–85 is the sweet spot for web photos — visually indistinguishable from the original but dramatically smaller. For thumbnails or previews, 50–60 works well.

Can I convert WebP back to JPEG/PNG?

This tool converts TO WebP. To convert WebP to other formats, use a dedicated converter or image editor.
