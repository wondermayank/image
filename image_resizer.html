Frequently asked questions

What photo size does SSC require?

SSC exams typically ask for a passport-style photo around 200×230 pixels, under 50KB, in JPG format on a light background. Always check the specific notification PDF, since exact limits vary by exam cycle.

What is the standard passport photo size in mm?

The standard passport photo size is 35mm × 45mm. At 300 DPI (the typical print resolution), that converts to roughly 413×531 pixels — this tool does that conversion automatically when you pick the mm unit.

How do I resize a photo to an exact KB size for an exam form?

Resizing dimensions alone doesn't control file size — that also depends on JPG compression. This tool's "target file size" option automatically adjusts compression quality until the output lands at or under your KB limit.

Should I crop or stretch my photo?

Crop is recommended for ID-style photos — it keeps your face proportions natural by cutting to the exact aspect ratio. Stretch can distort the image if your original photo's aspect ratio doesn't match the target, which exam portals may reject.
